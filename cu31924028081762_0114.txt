ABSTRAOT.

Notarial Instrument narrating that Friar Patrick Pulty‚ prior of the
convent of the Friars Preachers of Ayr, went personally, in presence of
notary and witnesses, to the messuage of John Orawfurde, laird of Kerse, to
distrain the lands of Kerse for fifty shillings of yearly rent, due to the said
prior and convent, of the Whitsunday term last past, and finding on the
ground of the said lands certain cows, he began to lead them away with
him, in default of payment of the said sum, of which cows, John M‘Kee,
servant, as he affirmed, of the aforesaid John Orawfurde‚ despoiled the said
prior, and led them back with him, in presence of John Grawfurde foresaid,
upon which the said prior asserted that because of the hindrance then
offered to him by the said John M‘Kee‚ he was not able at that time to
obtain his rent. Done on the said lands of Kerse, about eleven o’clock

before noon, 11th September 1460,
